# Event SignUps

SignUps let you create sign-up sheets for volunteer slots, items, meals, chaperones, rides, housing, and other opportunities within an event. Registrants and members sign up via a public link or while logged in. You can start from a ready-made template, add custom questions, share the sheet several ways, and run a login-free check-in kiosk at the event.

<Info>
**Platform**: Web only for creating and managing sheets. Public sign-up and check-in pages work on any device.
</Info>

## Overview

SignUps support:

- **Volunteer slots** — Time-based or capacity-limited slots (day, role, time, capacity)
- **Item / meal lists** — Bring-a-thing lists with quantities
- **Quick-start templates** — Pre-filled sheets built from data your event already has (menu, meal periods, housing locations, routes, vehicles)
- **Custom questions** — Ask each person extra questions when they sign up
- **Reminders** — Optional automated reminders to people who signed up
- **Sharing hub** — Public sign-up link, QR code, downloadable QR image, website embed, a leader "manage" link, and a check-in kiosk link
- **Roster management** — View who signed up, export, email the roster
- **Check-in** — A login-free kiosk for checking volunteers in and out at the event

## Accessing SignUps

1. Open the **Event Dashboard** for your event
2. On **Overview**, use the **Add a module** card to enable **SignUps** (if not already on)
3. Open the **SignUps** module from the rail

<Info>
SignUps must be enabled for the event before the section appears. You also need `logistics_signups_view` or admin permissions.
</Info>

## Creating a Sign-Up Sheet

Click **+ New sign-up sheet**. You'll see a **template gallery** — pick a template to pre-fill a sheet, or start from scratch.

### Start from a template

Templates pull from data your event already has, so you don't build slots by hand. If a module has no data yet, the sheet is still created with a sensible starting point and a note explaining what to add.

**General**
- **Volunteer slots** — A blank day / role / time / capacity sheet
- **Item / meal list** — A blank bring-a-thing list with quantities

**Meals**
- **Bring a dish** — Auto-fills a claim list from your event menu items
- **Cafeteria volunteers** — One serving shift per meal period on your schedule

**Housing**
- **Request housing** — People claim a spot from your housing locations
- **Chaperone sign-up** — A chaperone slot for each housing location (members only)
- **Host a home** — Volunteers offer space in their home

**Transportation**
- **Volunteer drivers** — Recruit drivers, one slot per route (members only)
- **Ride / shuttle sign-up** — Seats fill from your vehicles and routes

After you pick a template, the normal sheet builder opens with everything filled in — edit anything before saving.

### Build a sheet

Whether you start blank or from a template, configure:

- **Title** and description
- **Who can sign up** — **Mix** (guests can take open slots, members required for restricted slots), **Anyone with the link** (no login), or **Logged-in members only**. In **Mix** mode, use the per-row **Members** toggle to mark which slots require a member login.
- **Slots / items** — Time slots or item rows with capacity (or quantity) limits
- **Custom questions** — Click **+ Add question** to ask each person extra questions (for example "Vehicle & number of seats" or "Phone number"); mark questions required as needed
- **Reminders** — Choose an automated reminder schedule (or none)

Save the sheet.

## Sharing a Sign-Up Sheet

Open the sheet and click **Share** to open the sharing hub. Every link the sheet supports is in one place:

- **Sign-up link** — The public URL people use to sign up. Copy it, or use the **QR code** and **Download PNG** to print or display it, or grab the **Embed on your website** snippet.
- **Manage link (for a leader)** — Hand this to a ministry leader so they can edit slots, view the roster, and run check-in for this sheet — no account needed. (Admin only.)
- **Check-in kiosk link** — A login-free kiosk for checking volunteers in and out at the event. Open it on a tablet at the door. Use **Open kiosk** to launch it. (Admin only.)

Public sign-up URL format:

```
https://church.worshipbuddy.org/signup/?org={org_id}&event={event_id}&sheet={sheet_id}
```

## Signing Up (Registrant View)

On the public sign-up page, users can:

1. View available slots, items, and remaining capacity
2. Fill in their details and answer any custom questions
3. Claim a slot or item
4. Cancel their sign-up (if allowed)

Members logged into ChurchBuddy see their identity pre-filled. Members-only slots require a login even on a public link.

## Managing the Roster

From the SignUps section for each sheet:

- **Roster** — Toggle the inline roster view, grouped by day, with fill counts and each person's answers
- **Export** — Download sign-up data as a spreadsheet
- **Email** — Send the roster to coordinators (requires signups edit permission)
- **Check-in** — Open the check-in kiosk for this sheet
- **Edit / Delete sheet** — Modify or remove the sheet

## Check-In

SignUps includes a login-free **check-in kiosk** for the event, plus a manager console in the leader/portal view.

- **Kiosk** (open on a door tablet): people search for their name and check themselves in or out, and walk-ins can be added. If the sheet is linked to transportation, the kiosk can show a view-only live vehicle map.
- **Manager console** (in the leader manage view): the same attendance tools plus manager-only actions — manually add someone and, for transportation-linked sheets, drag-assign a person to a vehicle.

## Best Practices

1. **Start from a template** — Let the event's menu, routes, or housing pre-fill the sheet for you
2. **Set clear slot descriptions** — Help people know what they're signing up for
3. **Set appropriate capacity** — Prevent over-sign-up
4. **Ask only what you need** — Add custom questions sparingly to keep sign-up fast
5. **Share early** — Send links with event communications and turn on reminders
6. **Use the kiosk at the door** — Check volunteers in and out without logins
7. **Use mixed access carefully** — Members-only for internal volunteer roles; mixed for public item drives
